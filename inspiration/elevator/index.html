Lesson: Elevator Security
Apr 20, 2017
2 minutes read
  • design
  • inspiration
  • elevator
  • hci

During the nicer months in Montreal, I bike to work. We recently moved to a new building that has a parking garage below it, and a bike rack in the parking garage.

To get to my bike after work I:

  1. Take an elevator down to the basement
  2. Use my key fob to open a locked door
  3. Take a second elevator down a level

Between steps 2 and 3 something really nice happens. When I unlock the door, the elevator is called automagically. Most of the time the elevator doors are sliding open as I’m opening the security door and the elevator chimes as I’m walking into the hallway.

On the surface it’s a really simple piece of design, but I think it demonstrates a design concept that I need to keep in mind more often. Most of the time, users have goals. The focus of the design needs to be on the goal, rather than the specific task at hand.

Rather than just responding to the immediate need of the user to get past a locked door, the designer took into account that the user’s goal is to get to their car (or bike in my case). Unlocking the door completes the immediate task of getting past the security door, but calling the elevator helps advance the user even further towards getting to their goal.

Lesson for my future self: Remember where the user wants to go, and help them get there.
