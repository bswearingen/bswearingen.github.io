With the Trump candidacy and now presidency, the extreme, far right subset of the Reddit population has become increasingly vocal. Oftentimes these users will alternate between outright trolling and bad-faith “debating”. I found myself engaging with them too often because I’m a sucker. After spending some time arguing, I’d take some time to go through their post history to see what kind of people I was arguing with, and whether it was worth it. The vast majority of the time, the shibboleths or outright bigotry present there would have been enough to make me question engaging with them in the first place.

In a modern context, a “shibboleth” is a word or phrase that can be used to distinguish an in-group from an out-group. Jargon, in-jokes, and shared cultural memories can all serve as shibboleths, demonstrating that the person using them is properly a part of the in-group. With the semi-fractured nature of the Internet, we can even view the memes that a person uses as a type of shibboleth. If someone posts about having a “grilled Gary sandwich,” the odds are better than average that the poster is a vegan. It’s a fairly safe leap to make.

This same logic can be applied in a much more serious way to the memes and word-choice of the altright. If someone posts frequently about multiculturalism, white nationalism, and red pills, it’s a fair bet that they’re an engaged member in an altright space. In order to make my own life easier, I spent a few evenings writing a simple Reddit bot to crawl through users’ post histories on demand, letting me know when and where they’re using far right shibboleths.
