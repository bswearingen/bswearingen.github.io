Summer 2017: Not very technical, but productive

With the weather finally getting appropriate for fall in Montreal, I thought I’d post a brief update on my life. I had three major areas of focus this summer:

  • Immigration - my work permit expires in January, so Sophie and I have started the common-law spouse sponsorship process.
  • French - I took a French course at Concordia. Sophie and Sophie’s family think that my French has improved, even if it’s hard for me to see the progress at times.
  • C - Working on CoPiglet showed me how rusty my C skills were. I’ve spent some time working through Hacker Rank problems to bring them back to a reasonable level.

Minor areas of focus:

  • Javascript micro-project - I wanted to play around with a few APIs so I created @VeryGoodCatBot. I automated my one good joke on the Internet..
  • Open Source - Even though I only started this at the beginning of October, it was hot enough that I’m including it in “summer.” Minor contributions so far, but OptiKey seems like it’s a good project for me to contribute to. It hits a few interest areas (HCI, accessibility), and is written in C#, which is my main work language.

Exciting new addition to our small family:

  • Luna - The best and smallest tortie.
