CoPiglet Update: Hardware
Jun 10, 2017
2 minutes read
  • bike
  • backpack
  • idea
  • CoPiglet

Progress has slowed, and likely won’t be getting faster any time soon, and that’s OK.

My last blog post on what I’m now calling the CoPiglet (like copilot, but a pig, get it?) focused on the Android application. I’ve held off on posting a follow-up post focusing on the hardware present in the backpack, because it’s been slowly starting to undergo some major changes.

The Present

The current hardware in the backpack is:

  • an Arduino Uno
  • an HC-05 Bluetooth module
  • two coin vibration motors
  • a battery

They’re working well as a proof-of-concept, but the Arduino is overkill for the processing that the backpack actually needs to do. Here’s the pseudocode of what actually occurs:

if received == 'Left':
	signal(left)
elif received == 'Right':
	signal(right)
elif received == 'Both':
	signal(both)

While there’s a bit more logic than that to control the actual receiving of Bluetooth data, the length that the motors should be on, and assigning ‘left’ and ‘right’ to pins, there’s nothing that would require the processing power of an Arduino.

The Future

With that in mind, I’ve been moving towards a much less powerful, and therefore much more energy efficient, solution. Last month I bought a Bluegiga DKBLE dev kit, and have been slowly getting comfortable with the BLE113 module and their custom scripting language, BGScript.

The newness of it all has slowed down the rate at which I’m progressing, and it’s likely that the rate won’t be increasing in the near future. I’m going to be taking a French course after work a few days starting at the end of this month, so as soon as I get more familiar with the new hardware I’ll have less time to spend working with it.
